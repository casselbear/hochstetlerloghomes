A Beautiful Hochstetler Log Home

What makes a log home so special? Is it the security and strength of the massive timbers and beams? Or maybe the crackling, dancing flame in the huge fireplace? Or possible the beautiful warm color and intricate grain of the wood itself?

That specialness may be different for all of us. That's why we invite you to visit our models - the beautiful McKay as well as the Blackfork - and discover your own connection to a log home. You'll also want to take a tour of our mill and see how we carefully mill the logs that will ultimately be part of your home!

Whatever it is - it is as captivating and enduring as life itself. We feel a deep pride in knowing we have contributed to this time-honored method of construction and hope you share our feeling.

Serving the Entire United States and Beyond

Looking for a beautiful log home? Hochstetler Log Homes can take care of your needs throughout the United States and Canada!

The Northeastern United States

You'll find a log home crafted by Hochstetler Log Homes nestled throughout the rolling hills and mountains of Maine, New Hampshire, Vermont, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, New York, New Jersey, Connecticut and Pennsylvania.

The Midwestern United States

Located in Ohio, you'll find many of our log homes across the state. Plus, our plans are very popular in adjoining states of Michigan and Indiana. Addionally, our homes are commonly built throughout Wisconsin, Illinois, Minnesota, Iowa, Missouri, Nebraska, Kansas, North Dakota and South Dakota.

The Southern United States

The south isn't just for Antebellum, Georgian or French Colonial styles... you'll find plently of log homes by Hochstetler throughout the south. Members of the Hochstetler family of homeowners live in Virginia, West Virginia, Kentucky, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee and Arkansas. Plus head further south to Georgia, Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Louisiana and Texas. Delaware, Maryland and Oklahoma also feature fine log homes.

The Western United States

From the deserts to the mountains across the western USA, you'll find Hochstetler homes across Montana, Wyoming, Idaho, New Mexico, Arizona, Utah, Nevada, California, Colorado, Oregon and Washington.
